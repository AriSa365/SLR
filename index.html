Effect of dietary fibre supplementation on HbA1c in adults with type 2 diabetes: a systematic review
Meta-analysis
Reynolds AN, Akerman AP, Mann J · Lancet 2023
Pooled analysis of 27 RCTs (n = 3,144) showed dietary fibre supplementation significantly reduced HbA1c (MD −0.41%, 95% CI −0.57 to −0.25) without significant heterogeneity (I² = 38%).
Quality:  27 studies n = 3,144
Exercise interventions for depression in older adults: a network meta-analysis of 58 trials
Meta-analysis
Singh B, Olds T, Curtis R · BMJ 2023
Resistance training, yoga, and aerobic exercise all showed large effects on depressive symptoms. Network analysis suggests combined modalities may be most efficacious (SMD −1.04, 95% CI −1.26 to −0.82).
Quality:  58 studies n = 8,210
Telemedicine for management of chronic pain: systematic review and qualitative synthesis
Qualitative
Turk DC, Wilson HD, Cahana A · Pain 2023
Thematic synthesis of 14 qualitative studies identified patient autonomy, access to care, and therapeutic alliance as central themes. Barriers included technological literacy and connectivity in rural populations.
Quality:  14 studies Mixed methods
Antibiotic prophylaxis to prevent surgical site infection: Cochrane systematic review update
RCT
Webster J, Osborne S · Cochrane Database 2022
Updated Cochrane review of 263 RCTs concludes antibiotic prophylaxis reduces SSI risk (RR 0.56, 95% CI 0.52–0.60). Evidence is high certainty for colorectal procedures, moderate for orthopaedic.
Quality:  263 studies n = 48,701
Prevalence of burnout among physicians: a systematic review and meta-analysis update 2010–2024
Observational
Rotenstein LS, Torre M, Ramos MA · JAMA 2024
Pooled prevalence of overall burnout was 44.2% (95% CI 41.6%–46.9%) among 171,655 physicians across 47 countries, with emergency medicine, ICU, and oncology showing highest rates.
Quality:  182 studies n = 171,655
Mindfulness-based interventions for anxiety in children and adolescents: a systematic review
Cohort
Dunning DL, Griffiths K, Kuyken W · Psychol Med 2022
Among 33 studies (n = 3,666 young people aged 6–18), mindfulness interventions showed moderate effects on anxiety symptoms (g = 0.51, 95% CI 0.38–0.64). School-based programmes showed the most consistent effects.
Quality:  33 studies n = 3,666
Platform capabilities

Every stage of your review, covered

From protocol registration to forest plots, SystematicaHub provides structured tools that align with PRISMA 2020, Cochrane Handbook, and GRADE guidelines.

Multi-database search
Connect to PubMed, Embase, CENTRAL, PsycINFO, and CINAHL. Deduplicate records automatically and import via RIS or BibTeX.
Dual-blind screening
Assign screeners, resolve conflicts with a third reviewer, and track inter-rater reliability (Cohen's κ) at every stage of title, abstract, and full-text review.
Structured data extraction
Build custom extraction forms with field validation. Export to Excel, CSV, or directly to RevMan. Supports PICO, PICOS, and SPIDER frameworks.
Risk of bias assessment
Built-in tools for RoB 2.0, ROBINS-I, Newcastle–Ottawa Scale, CASP checklists, and GRADE evidence profiling — with traffic-light summary tables.
Meta-analysis & forest plots
Fixed and random effects models, subgroup analysis, sensitivity analysis, and publication bias tests (Egger, Begg, funnel plot). Export publication-ready figures.
Team collaboration
Invite co-reviewers, assign roles, leave inline comments, and track review progress in real time — with full audit trail and version history.
